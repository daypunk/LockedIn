# designer-senior

## Snapshot

Senior product designer or UI/UX designer with 7-12 years of experience.
Owns one or more product surface areas end-to-end: discovery research,
concept exploration, high-fidelity prototyping, design system contribution,
and post-launch measurement. May own or co-own design operations for a team
(component governance, tooling, design-to-engineering handoff process) at
larger organizations. Distinct from a UX researcher (who focuses on study
design and synthesis) or a motion designer (who focuses on animation) in that
this persona ships production UI and bridges user insight to shipped product.
Targets roles at companies where design quality is a competitive differentiator:
consumer apps, B2B SaaS with complex workflows, or companies rebuilding legacy
products with modern design systems.

## Skill cluster

Hard skills (8-12 items):
- Figma at advanced depth: auto-layout, component variants, interactive
  prototypes, dev mode, FigJam facilitation
- Design systems: component libraries, design tokens, Storybook collaboration
  with frontend engineers
- User research methods: moderated usability tests, unmoderated remote
  studies (UserTesting, Maze), semi-structured interviews, diary studies
- Quantitative UX methods: funnel analysis in product analytics (Amplitude,
  Mixpanel), A/B test design and interpretation
- Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA design compliance, color contrast audits,
  focus-state design, screen reader consideration at the design stage
- Prototyping: Figma interactive prototypes, ProtoPie for complex micro-
  interaction logic
- Design critique facilitation and structured feedback methods
- Journey mapping, service blueprinting, job-to-be-done framing
- Information architecture and navigation pattern design
- Handoff documentation that supports frontend engineers without repeated
  clarification cycles

Soft / cross-functional skills (4-6 items):
- PM collaboration: translating ambiguous product goals into a scoped design
  brief with measurable success criteria
- Engineering partnership: advocating for feasibility constraints early,
  reducing late-stage design rework
- Stakeholder alignment: facilitating design reviews that produce a decision,
  not just feedback
- Design operations: establishing shared vocabulary, critique cadence, and
  component governance policies for the design team

## Responsibility patterns

- Owns the design process for a major product surface: from initial research
  synthesis through shipped UI, including post-launch success metric tracking.
- Conducts and synthesizes usability research that directly informs design
  decisions, with findings documented and shared across PM, engineering, and
  leadership.
- Contributes production-ready components to the design system, maintaining
  variant completeness, documentation, and Figma-to-code parity with the
  frontend component library.
- Runs structured design critiques and A/B concept tests that reduce the
  number of post-launch iterations required to hit activation or retention
  targets.
- Defines and tracks UX success metrics (task completion rate, time-on-task,
  error rate, satisfaction score) for the surfaces they own, not just
  vanity engagement metrics.
- Facilitates design sprint or co-design sessions with cross-functional
  stakeholders, producing alignment artifacts (journey maps, decision logs)
  that persist beyond the session.
- Mentors mid-level designers through structured feedback on portfolio and
  process, contributing to team skill development.

## Tone guidance

Vocabulary register: outcome-led, narrative-friendly, research-grounded.
Bullets should ground design decisions in user evidence (research finding,
usability test result, metric shift) rather than describing the deliverable
alone. Avoid purely aesthetic or subjective framing. A senior designer's
resume should read like a product outcome story with a design lens.

Lean on: Owned, Shipped, Researched, Validated, Defined, Redesigned,
Reduced, Increased, Facilitated, Contributed, Established, Reframed.

Avoid: "user-centric" (overused to the point of meaninglessness), "elegant
design" (aesthetic claim without a defined criterion), "collaborated with
stakeholders" (vague; specify the outcome), "created wireframes and
prototypes" (deliverable list without the impact).

## Action verb cluster

Owned, Shipped, Researched, Validated, Defined, Redesigned, Reduced,
Increased, Facilitated, Contributed, Established, Reframed, Conducted,
Synthesized, Designed, Led, Prototyped, Documented

## Banned phrases (persona-specific additions to global banned_phrases.json)

- "user-centric" — overused; replace with evidence of user research method
  and the decision it informed
- "elegant design" — aesthetic subjective; replace with a usability metric
  or accessibility score
- "delightful experience" — vague; if you mean engagement, quantify it
- "design thinking" — methodology label without evidence; describe the
  specific research or ideation method used
- "pixel-perfect" — same as frontend; replace with design-token fidelity
  or visual regression test coverage
- "creative solutions" — self-complimentary without evidence; describe the
  constraint and the approach

## Persona fit scoring guidance

- `designer-senior` persona_fit >= 4 requires at least 1 research method
  named (usability test, survey, A/B test, interview synthesis) AND at least
  1 business or UX outcome metric (task completion rate, conversion lift,
  activation rate, NPS, error rate reduction).
- A resume full of deliverable descriptions (wireframes, prototypes, style
  guides) without outcome metrics cannot score above 3.0 on persona_fit.
- Design system ownership or component contribution with adoption evidence
  (squad count, engineer handoff time reduction) strongly elevates persona_fit.
- Absence of any research method citation is a cap at 3.5 for senior
  designers — research integration is a core expectation at this level.

## Quality bar examples

Before: "Redesigned the onboarding flow to improve user experience."
After: "Redesigned the 7-step onboarding flow based on 12 moderated usability
sessions; task completion rate rose from 48% to 71% and 7-day activation
increased by 19pp in the A/B test over 6 weeks."

Before: "Contributed to the design system."
After: "Contributed 24 production-ready Figma components to the core design
system, each paired with a Storybook story; reduced designer-to-engineer
redline clarification cycles from ~4 per component to under 1."

Before: "Worked with PM and engineering on the checkout experience."
After: "Owned the checkout redesign from discovery to launch: 3 rounds of
prototype testing with 24 participants identified 2 critical friction points
that the original spec missed; post-launch cart abandonment dropped by 14%."
