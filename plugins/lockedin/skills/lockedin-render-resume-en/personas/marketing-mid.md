# marketing-mid

## Snapshot

Mid-level growth or product marketing manager with 3-6 years of experience.
Owns a channel, funnel stage, or product audience segment end-to-end: builds
the content strategy, runs the A/B tests, reads the attribution data, and
iterates on lifecycle campaigns. Distinct from a field marketing specialist
(event-focused) or a brand manager (awareness-focused) in that this persona
drives measurable funnel outcomes (acquisition, activation, retention,
expansion). Targets growth-stage B2C or B2B SaaS companies where marketing
is expected to move business metrics directly, not just generate impressions.

## Skill cluster

Hard skills (8-12 items):
- Email and lifecycle marketing: ESP tools (Braze, Klaviyo, Iterable,
  Customer.io), segmentation logic, A/B test design
- Growth analytics: funnel analysis in Amplitude, Mixpanel, or Looker;
  cohort retention curves; conversion attribution
- Paid acquisition: Meta Ads, Google Ads, or LinkedIn campaign management
  at meaningful spend levels (>$10k/month)
- SEO: keyword research, content calendar, on-page optimization, backlink
  strategy — reporting through Search Console and Ahrefs / SEMrush
- Attribution modeling: last-touch vs. multi-touch, UTM hygiene, MMM
  awareness
- Content production: blog, case study, or landing page copy at conversion-
  oriented depth
- A/B testing: experiment design, statistical significance thresholds,
  MDE calculation
- Product launch coordination: GTM planning, release notes, in-app
  messaging, sales enablement
- CRM hygiene and lead-scoring logic (HubSpot, Salesforce basics)

Soft / cross-functional skills (4-6 items):
- Sales and CS partnership: translating market research and win/loss data
  into positioning updates for the sales team
- Product collaboration: providing user-language input for in-app copy,
  feature naming, and onboarding flows
- Stakeholder reporting: translating funnel metrics into an executive
  narrative that connects to revenue
- Prioritization under channel budget constraints: allocating spend across
  channels using contribution margin logic

## Responsibility patterns

- Owns a funnel stage or lifecycle stage from hypothesis through execution,
  tracking conversion rate and revenue contribution on a sprint cadence.
- Runs A/B tests on email subject lines, landing page copy, or in-app
  messaging CTAs; designs experiments with pre-specified MDE and significance
  thresholds, then documents and shares results across the team.
- Builds and maintains audience segmentation logic in the ESP / marketing
  automation platform, creating behavioral cohorts that improve message
  relevance and reduce unsubscribe rates.
- Manages a paid acquisition budget, allocating spend across channels by
  cost-per-acquisition and LTV-to-CAC ratio, reporting weekly to marketing
  leadership.
- Leads the content or SEO program for a topic cluster, producing or
  commissioning assets that drive organic pipeline and tracking keyword
  rank changes over a 90-day horizon.
- Coordinates product launch GTM plans across product, sales, and customer
  success, producing positioning docs, one-pagers, and in-app messaging
  copy on a defined timeline.
- Defines and tracks leading indicators (trial-to-paid conversion, email
  click-to-activation, feature adoption rate) that predict downstream revenue
  metrics, reporting divergence from targets proactively.

## Tone guidance

Vocabulary register: outcome-led, funnel-aware, data-referenced. Bullets
should connect a marketing action to a funnel metric (conversion rate, CPL,
CAC, LTV, activation rate, revenue). Avoid marketing fluff that doesn't
quantify impact. This persona bridges the marketing-to-revenue gap clearly.

Lean on: Drove, Launched, Increased, Reduced, Built, Designed, Ran,
Managed, Owned, Tested, Shipped, Grew, Optimized, Defined.

Avoid: "growth hacker" (buzzword with no durable meaning), "synergize"
(global banned phrase — already covered), "brand awareness campaign" without
a measurable reach or downstream metric, "created content" without specifying
the channel performance, "cross-functional collaboration" without the specific
outcome that collaboration produced.

## Action verb cluster

Drove, Launched, Increased, Reduced, Built, Designed, Owned, Tested,
Grew, Managed, Defined, Shipped, Ran, Optimized, Produced, Coordinated,
Segmented, Established

## Banned phrases (persona-specific additions to global banned_phrases.json)

- "growth hacker" — buzzword; name the specific channel or technique and
  its measured impact
- "brand awareness" without a metric — acceptable only with reach, CPM,
  or survey lift data
- "go-to-market strategy" without deliverables — describe the specific GTM
  artifacts and the launch outcome
- "content strategy" without channel and performance data — name the
  channel (email, SEO, paid), the content type, and a conversion or
  traffic metric
- "data-driven marketing" — tautological at this level; always describe
  the specific metric and decision it drove
- "social media presence" without metrics — name the platform, post cadence,
  and engagement or follower-growth rate

## Persona fit scoring guidance

- `marketing-mid` persona_fit >= 4 requires at least 1 funnel-stage metric
  (conversion rate, CAC, CPA, activation rate, trial-to-paid rate, MQL
  volume with cost) AND at least 1 specific channel or tooling mention
  (Braze, Klaviyo, Google Ads, Amplitude, HubSpot, Meta Ads).
- A resume that reads as communications or PR without funnel ownership or
  experiment evidence cannot score above 2.5 on persona_fit.
- A/B test design evidence (MDE, significance threshold, decision documented)
  strongly differentiates this persona from a mid-level marketing coordinator.
- Attribution methodology awareness (UTM hygiene, multi-touch attribution
  comments) is a plus signal for marketing-mid persona_fit at this level.

## Quality bar examples

Before: "Managed email marketing campaigns to improve engagement."
After: "Built a 5-step behavioral email drip for trial users in Braze,
segmented by in-app activation events; trial-to-paid conversion for the
cohort rose from 8.4% to 13.1% over the 60-day nurture window."

Before: "Ran A/B tests on landing pages."
After: "Ran 12 landing page A/B tests across 3 paid acquisition campaigns
(Google, Meta, LinkedIn), pre-specifying 95% significance threshold and
10% MDE; 4 winners shipped, reducing blended CAC from $214 to $167 over
two quarters."

Before: "Led content strategy for the blog."
After: "Owned the SEO content program for 3 product topic clusters; produced
or commissioned 28 articles over 6 months, growing organic trial signups
from that channel by 41% and pushing 6 target keywords from page 3 to page 1."
